About The Beach Lab

Welcome to The Beach Lab, a Fab(ulous) Lab(oratory) for research and development in digital fabrication and synthetic biology participant in a global network of interconnected Labs. In 2001 MITâ€™s Center for Bits and Atoms started the first Fab Lab as an outreach social innovation project in India. Since then the movement has spreaded over the world, helping local communities to develop a self-sustainable future through collaborative innovation, with fab labs doubling faster than Moore's Law.

The Beach Lab is a bleeding edge concept of mobile fab lab. It's the most advanced digital fabrication technology. Retrofitted in he most extreme off-road vehicle. Reaching the most remote regions and severe conditions. Powered by the Tesseract, a foldable masterpiece of structural engineering and seismic design applied to a lightweight cubic structure.

The Beach Lab is one of the Supernodes participant in Academany educational programs, delivering Fab Academy HTMAA (How to make almost anything) and Bio Academy HTGAA (How to grow almost anything) courses to any place on Earth.

Flip Flap: Open Source Mechanical Split Flap Display

Project Description:

Flip Flap is an open source mechanical Split Flap display like those in train station and airports.

This project was released in Maker Faire Rome 2014 and is going to be soon pushed to production through a kickstarter crowdfunding campaign.

Project Details:

  • Client: Beach Lab
  • Documentation and files: Beach Lab's Github
  • Date: October 2014

Light Cycle: Open Source Persistence of Vision Light Cycle

This project came after a 3 years old kid asked us to help him to become Tron.
